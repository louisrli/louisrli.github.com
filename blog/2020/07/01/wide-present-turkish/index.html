Simple Present Tense in Turkish

This post talks about the simple present tense in Turkish (also known as the wide present tense or aorist). The purpose of this article is not precisely to explain the tense itself, but to explain when you would use the wide present versus other similar tenses. While there are a number of English resources that explain the basics of the tense, as far as I can see, there are none that delve into various scenarios where using one tense over the other could be ambiguous for English speakers.

As a disclaimer, I’m an ongoing student of Turkish, but this is by no means meant to be a definitive guide for when to use one tense versus another. However, I do hope to provide and document a few heuristics.

Note: In my opinion, the “simple present” is a bit misleading of a name. Wide present (the literal translation of its Turkish designation, geniş zaman) is more reflective of the wide span of time that this tense can take.
